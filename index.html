Rohit Salla

Hello! I'm a Master's student in Computer Engineering at Virginia Tech, where I work on robot learning and machine learning — with a particular interest in retrieval systems and reinforcement learning reasoning. I'm currently working with Simon Stepputtis at TEALab.

Prior to my Master's, I was fortunate to do research across three labs that shaped how I think about perception, retrieval, and 3D scene understanding.

At NCCU Taiwan, I worked with Prof. Chih-Yi Chiu on token quantization for late-interaction retrieval and on multimodal recommendation systems. The retrieval line of work paired ViT image encoders with BERT text features over a FAISS index, with explicit diversity and fairness constraints in the re-ranking stage.

At IISc, I worked with Prof. Suresh Sundaram on transformer-based object detection. The main project was Hi-DETR — a hierarchical dense supervision detector built on a hybrid encoder with multi-scale deformable attention and task-aligned cross-attention, designed for small-object recall in cluttered AGV scenes (improving over RT-DETRv3 and SODETR). Beyond the detector, I built end-to-end perception stacks for autonomous ground vehicles, fusing LiDAR, radar, and camera streams and deploying them on embedded hardware via ROS.

At CMU, I worked with Prof. Min Xu on 3D segmentation — volumetric scene understanding and dense structure prediction from 3D inputs, with a focus on building models that remain robust under occlusion and scale variation.

§ I — Research Interests

Robot Learning & Reasoning

I'm broadly interested in building robot agents that can both learn and reason. The questions that draw me in: when should a policy think harder before it acts? How do we let agents transfer skills across tasks rather than re-learning from scratch? Can large language models serve as planners or reasoning modules for embodied control?

Concretely, my work spans diffusion policies for continuous control, prefix-optimal stopping in policy rollouts, exploration in linear RL, and the practical question of how to make policies adaptive in their compute usage. On the perception and language side, I work on retrieval-augmented systems — late-interaction retrieval, neuro-symbolic indexing for evolving corpora, and quantifying contradictions in multi-query LLM reasoning. The thread that ties it all together is knowing when a model has done enough.
